Version control is important in collaborative projects because it helps team members track changes and work together without overwriting each other's work. It keeps a history of all updates, making it easy to review or restore previous versions if needed. Version control also improves teamwork by allowing multiple people to contribute efficiently and resolve conflicts when changes overlap.
Git is better than managing files by hand because it saves every change you make automatically. It also makes it easier for people to work together and helps prevent files from being lost or accidentally overwritten.
